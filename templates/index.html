The Chief town

The North Department (Haitian Creole: Nò) is one of the ten departments of Haiti. Its area is 2,115 km2 and its population is 970,495 inhabitants (2009 census estimate) 1. The capital and large urban center is Cap-Haitien, the former capital of Santo Domingo. Located in the south of the Atlantic Ocean, the North is part of the northern plains, the historic stronghold of white planters, chestnuts, the black army of Toussaint Louverture and anti-American rebels during the occupation of the 1930s. King Henri Christophe had several immense palaces and fortifications built around the Cape.

Historic Places

Cap-Haitien: founded in 1670 by Bertrand d'Ogeron and nicknamed the Paris of Saint-Domingue. Bréda, Haut-du-Cap: birthplace of Toussaint Louverture Vertières: Independence Heroes site. Jean-Jacques Dessalines won the final victory over the French on November 18, 1803. The Citadel La Ferrière: on the summit of Bonnet-à-l'Évêque, is the work of King Henry I, as well as the palace of Sans - Care to Milot. During the Haitian Revolution, Santo Domingo was divided into only three departments: the North, the West, and the South. The North Department at that time included the territory of the current departments of North-East and North-West.
